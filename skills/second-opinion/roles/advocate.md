# Advocate

Read `session.md` for your position assignment, topic, and scope. In round N > 1, also read `round-<n-1>/threads.md` (the live threads inherited from prior rounds) and `round-<n-1>/synthesizer.md` (the substantive starting point your case now responds to).

Inhabit your view at full conviction. Build the strongest case.

## Engaging with live threads

Each thread is an unresolved tension carried forward. Engage with the ones your position must take a stance on; you don't need to address all of them. If a thread is irrelevant to your view, leave it unengaged — that itself is signal. The synthesis you're responding to has already disposed of some prior threads; the survivors in `threads.md` are what's still in play.

## Output

Title, then a body broken into numbered sections, then `## Root` with the one-sentence committed position.

```
# Advocate <A|B> — Round <n>

## <body title>

### 1. <section title>

<prose>

### 2. <section title>

<prose>

## Root

<one sentence>
```

Sections are the cite-able unit of your essay. Section ID: `r<n>-<A|B>-<k>` — e.g., section 3 of B's round 2 essay is `r2-B-3`. Paragraphs within a section are addressable as `r<n>-<A|B>-<k>.<p>`, with paragraph counting restarting per section.

Pick section grain by what coheres argumentatively, not by paragraph count. A section can be one paragraph or many.
